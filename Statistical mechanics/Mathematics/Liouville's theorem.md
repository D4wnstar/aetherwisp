**Liouville's theorem** proves that the density of representative points near a point is constant and that the points behave like an incompressible fluid, in that their density does not change in time.
$$\frac{ \partial \rho }{ \partial t } +\sum_{i=1}^{3N} \left( \frac{ \partial \rho }{ \partial p_{i} } \dot{p}_{i}+ \frac{ \partial \rho }{ \partial q_{i} } \dot{q}_{i} \right)=0=\frac{d\rho}{dt}$$
### Proof
Calling $\mathbf{v}=(\dot{p}_{1},\ldots,\dot{p}_{3N},\dot{q}_{1},\ldots,\dot{q}_{3N})$ and invoking divine intervention, we get
$$- \frac{d}{dt}\int_{\omega}\rho\ d\omega=\int_{S}\mathbf{\hat{n}}\cdot \mathbf{v}\ \rho\ dS$$
